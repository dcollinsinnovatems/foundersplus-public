Surviving the Valley of Death

Local Retail Outlet: "Delaney’s General Goods"

Delaney opened a neighborhood retail shop in Month 1 with $60K in personal savings. Rent, payroll, and inventory cost her about $15K/month, and she expected foot traffic by Month 4. But an unexpected road closure hurt early sales. Revenue trickled in ($3K–$5K/month), forcing her to run promotions and cut hours. A local feature in Month 7 brought a short-lived spike. By Month 12, she secured a $50K line of credit, just as revenues finally hit $12K/month. Still, summer months dragged — foot traffic dropped, and HVAC repairs blew her budget. Month 18 marked a turning point: she added delivery via a local startup, and Q4 revenue jumped to $20K+. By Month 30, she stabilized with $25K/month and leaned into e-commerce. The cash line dipped below -$180K but recovered steadily. Her Valley of Death lasted nearly 20 months, but discipline, local press, and smart timing pulled her through.
