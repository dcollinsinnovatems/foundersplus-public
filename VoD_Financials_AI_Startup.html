Surviving the Valley of Death

AI Overlay Startup: "EchoLens"

EchoLens built a Chrome extension using OpenAI and Gemini to simplify meeting transcription and insight tagging. Pre-seed grant funding ($100K) supported 9 months of solo founder development. They spent little — $3K/month — but earned nothing early on. A Product Hunt launch in Month 5 yielded 8,000 signups but no monetization. By Month 10, they offered paid tiers, but uptake was weak. Revenue jumped in Month 13 after a viral LinkedIn post and a podcast mention, bringing $18K MRR for two months. That dropped to $6K after Google Workspace changes disrupted their integration. Meanwhile, a surprise AWS bill and compliance consultant pushed expenses up. By Month 20, a small angel check ($80K) revived growth. They hit $25K/month revenue by Month 30 with a revamped team and stable infrastructure. Their chart shows long flat revenue, a sharp spike, a dip, and recovery — classic AI overlay turbulence. Ending cash hovers near $0, but they survive with slim margins.
