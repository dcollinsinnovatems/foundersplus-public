Surviving the Valley of Death

PaaS Technology Company: "TrackLayer"

TrackLayer spent its first year building a complex platform-as-a-service for IoT fleet managers. With no revenue model until Month 16, the team raised $250K in Month 1 and burned ~$30K/month on developers, product, and pilots. A proof-of-concept with a Mississippi distributor in Month 9 validated demand, but integration delays ate 3 more months. Expenses peaked in Month 14 with a full-time CTO and server upgrades. They launched paid pilots in Month 17, earning $8K–$10K/month, scaling to $45K/month by Month 30 as partners rolled out across 3 states. A grant from InnovateMS in Month 24 helped extend runway. Cash dropped below -$240K before recovery. Their chart looks like a U-curve with a mid-surge cash bump from grants. Net cash doesn’t go positive until Month 33 — but they survive and show strong slope upward. Classic Valley of Death with deep capital need and long delay to meaningful revenue.
