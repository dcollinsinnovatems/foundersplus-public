Surviving the Valley of Death

Consulting Firm: "Vector Advisory Co."

Three ex-corporate consultants launched Vector with strong networks but zero recurring revenue. They had runway for 8 months, estimating $12K in monthly burn. Month 2 brought a $40K contract — but it was slow to pay. Revenues arrived in clumps: $0, then $25K, then $8K. A Month 6 conference helped land two retainer clients, but onboarding delays meant they stayed cash-negative. By Month 9, their net cash sat at -$75K. One founder exited, reducing burn. In Month 10, a nonprofit engagement unexpectedly doubled scope, and revenue hit $35K for three months. The team reinvested in legal, web updates, and a part-time coordinator. Burn stayed high, but revenue steadied. By Month 18, they averaged $28K/month in revenue with lower volatility. They became cash-positive in Month 21 and broke $500K total revenue by Month 36. The chart’s revenue line zigzags, while expenses climb slowly but steady. Net cash hovers below zero until late — a typical bootstrapped consulting profile.
