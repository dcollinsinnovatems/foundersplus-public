Surviving the Valley of Death

SaaS University Spinout: "NeuroProof"

Spun out of a university lab, NeuroProof commercialized brainwave-optimized learning software. The tech was promising, but the market wasn’t ready. Month 1 began with $75K in licensing fees, $50K in state-matching funds, and two grad students. Expenses were modest ($12K/month) but inconsistent — IRB costs, IP attorneys, and dev agency spikes in Months 5, 11, and 19. They projected customer pilots by Month 12, but institutional sales cycles dragged. A university partner helped test in Month 14, then ghosted. Revenue didn’t materialize until Month 22 — a tiny $4K pilot. However, Month 26 brought a surprise: a K–12 edtech accelerator chose them, awarding $100K and introducing school district buyers. Revenue jumped to $20K/month by Month 30. The graph is flat, dips down, then jaggedly climbs. Ending cash is just under $80K, but the story is one of survival through grant stacking, delayed fit, and sudden momentum.
